2016 season review

After a strong start to the campaign the wins kept coming in May, June and July, culminating in Chris Froome's historic Tour de France hat-trick.

Having won the Criterium du Dauphine in swashbuckling fashion the 31 year old turned up to the Tour in top form and produced a text book performance to retain his title - and claim a third Tour de France victory.

May started with the Giro d'Italia and all Team Sky eyes were on Mikel Landa, who led our charge in Italy. The Spaniard started strongly, moving up to 10th ahead of the potentially tricky stage nine time trial.

Defying pre-stage predictions Landa ripped up the script and produced one of the rides of his career to put time into almost all of his key rivals in treacherous conditions around Chianti and move up to eighth overall.
